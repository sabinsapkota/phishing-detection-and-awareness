Phishing Awareness Training

Learn to identify and avoid phishing attacks

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack that uses disguised email as a weapon. The goal is to trick the email recipient into believing that the message is something they want or need.

  • Fake login pages
  • Malicious attachments
  • Urgent requests
Common Signs
  • Suspicious sender Check email addresses carefully
  • Odd links Hover to preview URLs
  • Poor spelling Grammar mistakes
  • Urgency "Act now!" pressure
Best Practices
  1. Verify sender identity
  2. Don't click suspicious links
  3. Check for HTTPS on websites
  4. Use multi-factor authentication
  5. Report suspicious emails
  6. Keep software updated
Interactive Examples
Suspicious Email Example:

From: security@amaz0n-security.com

Subject: Urgent: Account Suspension Warning!

Dear Customer,

Your account has been compromised! Click here immediately to verify your information or your account will be PERMANENTLY DELETED!!!

Verify Now

Misspelled domain (amaz0n), urgency, threats
Legitimate Email Example:

From: noreply@amazon.com

Subject: Your Amazon order #123-4567890

Hello John,

Thank you for your order. Your package will arrive on Tuesday. Track your shipment here.

Track Order

Correct domain, specific details, no urgency
